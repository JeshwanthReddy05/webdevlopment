Historical Places in India

The Taj Mahal, an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the south bank of the Yamuna river in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan, to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal. learn more

Qutub Minar, a soaring 73-meter tower of victory, built in 1193 by Qutab-ud-din Aibak. It is one of the tallest minarets in the world and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. learn more

The Ajanta Caves, a series of 30 rock-cut Buddhist cave monuments dating from the 2nd century BCE to about 480 CE. They are located in the Aurangabad district of Maharashtra state in India. learn more
